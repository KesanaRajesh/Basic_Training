Dihydrogen Monoxide

Dihydrogen monoxide, also known as hydroxyl acid or DHMO, is a dangerous substance that:

  • 1.Is the major component of acid rain.
  • 2.Contributes to the greenhouse effect.
  • 3.May cause severe burns.
  • 4.Is fatal if inhaled.
  • 5.Contributes to the erosion of our natural landscape.
  • 6.Accelerates corrosion and rusting of many metals.
  • 7.May cause electrical failures and decreased effectiveness of automobile brakes.
  • 8.Has been found in excised tumors of terminal cancer patients.

Even after washing, produce remains contaminated by this chemical.

1The more common chemical formula is H2O.
2Source: Coalition to Ban Dihydrogen Monoxide.
